Impact of Immunization on Disease Incidence Rates

A Data-Driven Analysis of Diphtheria, Measles, and Hepatitis

Vaccination plays a crucial role in preventing infectious diseases and reducing their incidence rates. This analysis focuses on three significant diseases: diphtheria, measles, and hepatitis. By examining immunization and incidence data, we aim to highlight the impact of vaccination efforts over time and underscore the importance of maintaining high immunization coverage to protect public health.

Background:

Over the past three decades, public health initiatives have significantly boosted vaccination rates. These linecharts vividly illustrate the relationship between the immunization coverage and the incidence of three different diseases. These linecharts showcase trends that are counterintuitive and really make the viewer question how effective immunization is when compared to other factors.

Background:

This scatterplot underscores the dynamic interplay between immunization coverage and disease prevalence. By visualizing individual data points, it allows us to discern patterns and anomalies in the data.
